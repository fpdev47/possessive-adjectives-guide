Possessive Adjectives

Los adjetivos posesivos — cuándo usarlos y cómo no confundirlos

🧠 What are Possessive Adjectives? Theory

Possessive adjectives tell us who something belongs to. They always go before a noun.

Los adjetivos posesivos indican a quién pertenece algo. Siempre van antes del sustantivo.


✅ my book  |  her car  |  their house

✅ mi libro  |  su carro (de ella)  |  su/sus casa (de ellos)

⚠️ The Big Confusion: its vs it's

its
🔒 Possession / Posesión
= "su" — pertenece a algo o a un animal
it's
🔗 Contraction / Contracción
= "it is" o "it has" — siempre se puede expandir

🤔 How do I know which one to use? / ¿Cómo sé cuál usar?

Use it's when you mean "It is..." or "It has..."
Usa it's cuando quieres decir "Es..." o "Ha..."
🌤️ "Es un día hermoso."
→ It's a beautiful day. (= It is a beautiful day)
📚 "Es un libro increíble."
→ It's an amazing book. (= It is an amazing book)
⏰ "Son las 3 de la tarde."
→ It's 3 PM. (= It is 3 PM)
🥶 "Hace frío hoy."
→ It's cold today. (= It is cold today)
Use its when something belongs to it
Usa its cuando algo le pertenece a una cosa o animal (= "su")
📖 "Sus páginas son interesantes."
→ Its pages are interesting. (pages belong to the book)
🐶 "Su cola es muy larga."
→ Its tail is very long. (the tail belongs to the dog)
🏢 "Su nombre es muy famoso." (hablando de una empresa)
→ Its name is very famous. (the name belongs to the company)
📱 "Su pantalla está rota." (hablando del teléfono)
→ Its screen is broken. (the screen belongs to the phone)

⚠️ Quick Note / Nota rápida: it has

You may also see it's as a contraction of "it has" — for example: "It's been a long day." (= It has been a long day.)

También puedes ver it's como contracción de "it has" — por ejemplo: "Ha sido un día largo."

📌 This belongs to the Present Perfect tense — a verb tense we will study later! For now, just remember: if you can replace it's with "it has" and it makes sense, it's still a contraction (with apostrophe ✅).

Esto pertenece al tiempo verbal Present Perfect — ¡un tiempo que estudiaremos más adelante! Por ahora, recuerda: si puedes reemplazar it's con "it has" y tiene sentido, sigue siendo una contracción (con apóstrofe ✅).

🔑 The Golden Test / La prueba de oro

Replace it's / its with "it is". Does the sentence still make sense?

Reemplaza it's / its con "it is". ¿La oración sigue teniendo sentido?

✅ "It's cold." → "It is cold." ✔️ Makes sense → use it's
❌ "The dog wags its tail." → "The dog wags it is tail." ✖️ Doesn't make sense → use its

💡 Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Wrong✅ CorrectWhy / Por qué
She lost hers keys.She lost her keys."hers" is a possessive pronoun, not adjective
It's color is red.Its color is red.No apostrophe for possession
Their are nice.They are nice. / Their car is nice.their needs a noun after it
I love my's job.I love my job.Never add 's to possessive adjectives

📌 Accent Marks in English / Tildes en inglés

English doesn't normally use accent marks. When spelling a name or word that has one, we describe it:

El inglés normalmente no usa tildes. Cuando deletreamos un nombre que tiene una, la describimos:

📖 Types of accent marks / Tipos de tildes

Acute ( ´ ) → "There's an acute accent mark on the i."
"Hay una tilde aguda en la i."
Grave ( ` ) → "There's a grave accent mark on the e."
"Hay una tilde grave en la e."

📌 "Spell" vs "Spelling" — What's the difference?

WordUseExample
to spell (verb) Action — to say or write the letters of a word

💡 Quick rule / Regla rápida

Use spell when talking about the action: "Can you spell it?"
Use spelling when talking about the result or the subject: "My spelling is improving!"

Usa spell para la acción: "¿Puedes deletrearlo?" · Usa spelling para el resultado o el tema: "¡Mi ortografía está mejorando!"
